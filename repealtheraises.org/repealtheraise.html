About the Petition

In late 2023, Warren’s part-time City Council passed a law doubling their own salaries and tying future raises to the city's highest paid employee. This Citizens’ Initiative seeks to:

  • Set Council pay to a reasonable amount
  • Freeze all elected officials' pay at current levels
  • Require all future pay changes to be decided by the voters

We need over 1,000 signatures by July 31, 2025 to place this on the November ballot.

FAQ

Q: Is it true that these political pay raises were needed to give prosecutors in the law department a raise?
A: No. Warren City Council has granted raises to prosecutors and other city employees in the past without increasing their own salaries. This claim is misleading.

Q: Does this initiative affect union contracts or city employees?
A: No. It only affects elected officials—not union or city employees.

Q: How do Warren’s elected official salaries compare to similar cities?
A: Council members in Warren make nearly 3 times more than those in Mansfield, often used for comparison in police negotiations.

Q: Does this initiative correct the current pay structure?
A: Yes. It freezes elected officials’ pay and eliminates unethical automatic increases.

Q: Is Warren the only city with this automatic raise structure?
A: Yes. This initiative will fix that and return fairness to government pay.

Q: What does signing the petition do?
A: It puts the initiative on the November 4, 2025 general election ballot.
